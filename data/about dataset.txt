                                                 About Dataset

The Diabetes prediction dataset is a collection of medical and demographic data from patients, along with their diabetes status (positive or negative). The data includes features such as age, gender, body mass index (BMI), hypertension, heart disease, smoking history, HbA1c level, and blood glucose level. This dataset can be used to build machine learning models to predict diabetes in patients based on their medical history and demographic information. This can be useful for healthcare professionals in identifying patients who may be at risk of developing diabetes and in developing personalized treatment plans. Additionally, the dataset can be used by researchers to explore the relationships between various medical and demographic factors and the likelihood of developing diabetes

COLUMNS:-

1. Age

    Represents the age of the patient (usually in years).
    Type: Numerical
    Importance:
    Diabetes risk generally increases with age.

2. Gender

    Indicates the biological sex of the patient.
    Type: Categorical (Male, Female, sometimes Other)
    Importance:
    Some studies show different diabetes risk patterns between genders.

3. Body Mass Index (BMI)

    A measure of body fat based on height and weight.
    Formula:

4. Hypertension
    
    Indicates whether the patient has high blood pressure.
    Type: Binary (0 = No, 1 = Yes)
    Importance:
    Strongly linked with Type 2 diabetes risk

5. Heart Disease

    Shows whether the patient has a history of heart disease.
    Type: Binary (0 = No, 1 = Yes)
    Importance:
    Cardiovascular issues often co-occur with diabetes

6. Smoking History

    Describes the patient’s smoking habits.
    Type: Categorical (examples):
    never
    former
    current
    not current
    Importance:
    Smoking affects insulin resistance and overall health

7. HbA1c Level (Glycated Hemoglobin)

    Measures average blood sugar over the past 2–3 months.
    Type: Numerical
    Typical interpretation:
    Normal: < 5.7%
    Prediabetes: 5.7% – 6.4%
    Diabetes: ≥ 6.5%
    Importance:
    One of the most critical indicators of diabetes

8. Blood Glucose Level

    Represents the current blood sugar level.
    Type: Numerical (mg/dL)
    Importance:
    High glucose levels → direct indicator of diabetes

9. Diabetes (Target Variable)

    Indicates whether the patient has diabetes.
    Type: Binary:
    0 = No diabetes
    1 = Diabetes
    Importance:
    This is what your ML model will predict

🧠 Summary

Input features: Age, Gender, BMI, Hypertension, Heart Disease, Smoking History, HbA1c, Glucose
Output/Label: Diabetes
Goal: Predict whether a person is diabetic based on health data
